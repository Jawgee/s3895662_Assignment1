Project

Overview

My IT project idea is a portable water quality sensor and temperature sensor. This device will allow average people to test the quality of possibly contaminated water or stream water in order to prevent any complications that may arise with the use of the water in question. It will also contain a temperature sensor as a bonus in the case of needing specific temperatures for certain projects. Finding out the quality of water may help with understanding how different events affect the quality of water sources and then further finding solutions to stop the pollution of these waters.

Motivation

“Some 80 percent of the world’s wastewater is dumped—largely untreated—back into the environment, polluting rivers, lakes, and oceans.” So, it is very important that we are able to test the water in local streams and other water sources to prevent the consumption or use of untreated or polluted water. This project will be useful for casual detection of pollutants that may be in water sources. As I have some experience using Arduino technology, this might be relatively easy to create. It should also be cheap as parts are small and easy to use.

Description

This product will feature a conductivity sensor which will test the purity of the water (more ionic materials in the water will raise conductivity), a temperature sensor that will measure the temperature of the water and the information will be displayed on a seven-segment display or an LCD display. To create a conductivity sensor, a plastic tube or something similar will be used with two wires soldered to nichrome wires on opposite sides of the tube. Electrical tape will be used to cover the wires for safety and a bit of the wires will be exposed to be used. The other ends of the wires will be plugged into the Arduino board and a 9V battery will be connected in order to be made portable. Most streams have a conductivity between 0.05 and 1.5 volts with freshwater streams between 0.15 and 0.5 volts. The voltage readings will be shown on a connected laptop.


As for the temperature sensor, it will be soldered to some wires which will be plugged into a breadboard. A seven-segment display will also be connected and possibly an LCD display (it may be difficult to use an LCD display to display this information mainly for the fact that there will need to be a lot of soldering). The seven-segment display will display the temperature of the water when the temperature sensor is placed in the water.


A possible case might be either laser cut or 3d printed big enough to hold all the parts with both conductivity and temperature sensor exposed from two holes in the case. Also, the way things would need to fit in the case would need to be considered since parts shouldn’t be damaged from shaking the case around and should be seated with little movement. I’d imagine that it would be convenient to be able to open the case quite easily so a possible click system can be made or even drilling in screws and unscrewing them when access is needed will suffice if a click system is not possible/too difficult. If implementing an LCD display it may be hard to manage space in the case as it will need to be specifically in a position and a hole must be cut for it.


There is a possibility that more batteries can be connected if the device loses power quite quickly. However, I imagine something as simple as this device wouldn’t require much power but if the device were to be left running for extended periods of time it may be smart to include a battery pack.

There has also been some thought into using a turbidity sensor rather than a conductivity sensor for the shear fact that it detects the total suspended solids in the water and is a direct indicator of polluted water compared to the conductivity only indicating how “pure” the water is which in turn can still determine the pollution of water to a certain degree.

Tools and Technologies

The software that will be used is the Arduino IDE which is written in C++ and will be what I will use to code the program and save it onto an Arduino board to execute the desired outcome. I will require a plastic rod or tube for the conductivity sensor and different connectors and Nichrome wire. I will also require a soldering iron to solder wires together. A DS18B20 temperature sensor will also be used which is also waterproof which is required for detecting the temperature of the water. A breadboard will also be needed to connect a seven-segment display or an LCD display which will display results in real time. A turbidity sensor may also be used for the detection of pollutants if the makeshift conductivity sensor is not enough. Possibly need a laser cutter or a 3d printer to create a case for the parts. Various tools for building the case might be needed as well.

Skills Required

I would need to have sound knowledge in using the Arduino software to code the program which I have a fair amount of knowledge on how to use it. I would also need to know how to physically connect parts properly in order to avoid any faults and ultimately make the circuit work as intended. It is relatively easy to find example codes from the Arduino website if assistance for understanding how a part works. Overall, I may need to brush up on my skills using the Arduino technology.

Outcome

Ultimately the outcome of this project is to effectively identify the quality of different water sources and their temperatures. This will allow for further understanding as to why and how the quality of the water is this way. But not only will this project help identify the quality of water but will allow a portable solution for any individual to carry with them. This project will ensure that there will be less safety issues with the consumption and overall use of water in streams and other places.
